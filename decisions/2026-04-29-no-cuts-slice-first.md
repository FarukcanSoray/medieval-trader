# No cuts to design scope; use slice-first with decision/session archival

## Decision
Reject the Scope Critic's recommendation to cut items from the design brief. Full scope holds. The construction strategy is **slice-first**: build the leanest end-to-end loop first, then deepen each system across sessions. **DecisionScribe** and **SessionSummarizer** are load-bearing parts of the development process, carrying deferred work and rationale across sessions.

## Reasoning
The user said verbatim: *"I don't accept the cuts. Vertical-slice-first approach is ok, we will use our decision scriber and session summarizer subagents to keep the state for later loops."*

The user accepts the Critic's scope concern (the "What's in" list is a 9–12 month shape described as 3–6 months) and addresses it via process — slice-first construction + archival — rather than by reducing the design ambition. The bet is that disciplined slicing and rigorous documentation will keep the integration-tax risk manageable across a longer timeline.

## Alternatives considered
The Critic recommended Reduce / Cut / Defer on most items in "What's in":

- Three of four encounter types (deferred).
- Supplies and cart capacity (cut from v1).
- Lifetime route history and full ledger on death screen (reduced).

User rejected all of these as scope reductions; accepted them only as *construction sequencing* — i.e. they are not in the slice, but they remain in project scope.

## Confidence
High. Explicit user statement; sets the construction strategy and the role of the archival agents for the remainder of the project.
