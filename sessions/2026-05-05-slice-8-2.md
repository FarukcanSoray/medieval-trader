## Decisions

- [[2026-05-05-slice-8-2-drain-conservation-composed]] -- drain + partial conservation composed in one slice (user override of Critic sequencing); proportional-to-fill drain so `base_demand_decay_rate` cancels in equilibrium.
- [[2026-05-05-slice-8-2-schema-v8-strict-reject]] -- v7 saves rejected on load; extends slice-8.1 precedent.
- [[2026-05-05-slice-8-2-conservation-rng-per-node-counter]] -- conservation RNG seed disambiguated by per-`NodeState` `sell_seed_counter`; persists across save/load.
- [[2026-05-05-slice-8-2-decrement-cap-permanent-separate-mutator]] -- per-tick consumption and permanent cap erosion are distinct `WorldState` verbs.
- [[2026-05-05-slice-8-2-1-same-node-shadow-permanent-gate]] -- `max(0, sell - buy) <= cheapest_edge_cost` codified as permanent pillar 1 gate; cross-node spread reframed to pillar 2 only.
- [[2026-05-05-slice-8-2-1-cross-node-floor-lowered]] -- cross-node legibility floor 0.40 -> 0.25 (0.40 became mathematically unreachable under shadow-respecting ratios).
- [[2026-05-05-slice-8-2-1-producer-demand-structurally-zero]] -- `DEMAND_DECAY_MULT_PRODUCER = 0.0` keeps producer pools at 0 forever; cleanest of three implementation options.
- [[2026-05-05-slice-8-2-1-consumer-drain-empirical-tuning]] -- consumer drain mult 11.5 (empirical) over 10.0 (analytical); empirical wins near a binding constraint.

## Notes

The slice-8.2.1 retune surfaced a structural finding worth carrying forward: the kernel-collision shadow (cheapest edge cost / iron base price = 9/22 ~= 0.41) imposes a **hard ceiling on cross-node consumer ratio** under the locked formula and current goods catalogue. This is now a permanent project constraint until iron's `base_price` changes or travel cost rises -- both deferred as out-of-scope balance work.
