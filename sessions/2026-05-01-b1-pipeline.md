## Notes

The session's most architecturally durable artifact wasn't the harness or the runbook individually — it was the **harness/runbook split as a structural consequence of the post-load function constraint**. Designer's mapping pass surfaced that 5 of 12 failure modes are catchable by a pure post-load `(trader, world)` function and 7 are fundamentally pre/post comparisons. That isn't a compromise — it's the correct decomposition. Future automated/manual test splits should start by asking which side the constraint actually places each check on, rather than defaulting to "automated good, manual bad."

Director also overruled Architect on warn-only-vs-regenerate with a framing that generalizes beyond B1: *"warn-only is functionally indistinguishable from not having the harness."* Any defensive check whose failure path is "log and continue" hollows out the invariant it exists to defend. Worth remembering when other defensive checks surface in future rounds.
